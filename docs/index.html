03 — Faith

Overwhelmingly Muslim — the exceptions are almost all guest workers

Saudi Arabia's census does not ask about religion, so no official breakdown exists. The figures below are third-party demographic estimates (Pew Research / ARDA-style modeling), shown as an approximate picture rather than a census fact.

~93%of residents are estimated to be Muslim
Among Saudi citizens specifically, an estimated 85–90% are Sunni and 10–15% Shia (mostly Twelver, concentrated in the Eastern Province), plus small Ismaili and Zaydi communities. Nearly all Christians, Hindus and Buddhists in the Kingdom are foreign workers on residency permits, not citizens — Hindu residents in particular track closely with the South Asian labor migration visible in the regional chart above.

Caveats worth keeping in mind: city-level population is only ever as fresh as the last census (2022) — there is no official annual city series. Regional (not city) breakdowns are annual through 2022 only, since the 2022 census reset the series. Religion is modeled, not measured. Historical population/GDP charts above are annual/quarterly — the true official resolution — deliberately not interpolated into fake monthly points. The "Today" panel above is the only genuinely daily-resolution data on this page.
